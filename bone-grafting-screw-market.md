<p><p>The bone grafting screw market consists of three main types: stainless steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable screws. Stainless steel screws offer robustness and biocompatibility, often used in non-load bearing applications. Titanium screws are favored for their strength, lightweight nature, and excellent integration with bone, making them suitable for load-bearing scenarios. Bioabsorbable screws are increasingly popular for their ability to naturally dissolve over time, minimizing the need for a second surgery and reducing foreign material in the body.</p></p>
